Samosa

Samosa is a popular Indian snack that features a crispy, golden pastry filled with spiced potatoes, peas, and sometimes meat. It's deep-fried to perfection, offering a satisfying crunch with every bite. Typically served with chutneys, samosas are enjoyed across South Asia and have become a favorite street food around the world.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour (maida)
  • Boiled potatoes
  • Green peas
  • Green chili
  • Ginger
  • Spices (cumin, garam masala, coriander powder, etc.)
  • Salt
  • Oil
  • Water

Steps

  1. Prepare dough with maida, salt, oil, and water; rest for 30 mins.
  2. Boil and mash potatoes, then saut√© with peas, chili, and spices.
  3. Divide dough into balls and roll into thin oval shapes.
  4. Cut each oval in half and form a cone shape with one half.
  5. Fill the cone with potato mixture and seal the edges with water.
  6. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
  7. Deep-fry samosas until golden and crispy.
  8. Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  9. Serve hot with tamarind or mint chutney.
