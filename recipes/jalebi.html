Jalebi

Jalebi is a popular Indian sweet known for its spiral shape, crispy texture, and syrupy sweetness. Made by deep-frying fermented batter into coils and then soaking them in sugar syrup, jalebi is often enjoyed hot and fresh during festivals, weddings, or as a special treat with breakfast. Its golden-orange color and sticky exterior make it instantly recognizable, while its rich taste brings joy across all age groups.

Home

Ingredients

  • Maida – 1 cup
  • Curd – ½ cup
  • Cornflour – 2 tbsp
  • Baking soda – pinch
  • Water – as needed
  • Sugar – 1 cup

Steps

  • Mix maida, cornflour, baking soda, curd, and water to make a smooth batter.
  • Let the batter ferment for 6–8 hours.
  • Boil sugar and water to make syrup with 1-string consistency.
  • Add lemon juice and optional flavorings to the syrup.
  • Pour the batter into a piping bag or bottle.
  • Heat oil or ghee in a flat pan.
  • Pipe spiral shapes into the hot oil and fry till golden.
  • Dip fried jalebis into warm sugar syrup for 30–60 seconds.
  • Remove and serve hot.
